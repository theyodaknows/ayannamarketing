Origin Story

From intern
to industry.

Ayanna Henderson didn't start with a corner office or a Fortune 500 client list. She started as a high school production intern in Los Angeles — sitting in rooms where decisions were being made, watching how content, strategy, and brand identity worked together to build something people actually cared about.

"I was in rooms that most people spend their entire career trying to get into. I paid attention."

Over the next decade and a half, Ayanna built one of the most distinctive marketing careers in Los Angeles. She scaled Steve Harvey's social media presence from 15 million to over 70 million followers — managing digital strategy across his full brand portfolio while productions filmed, awards shows aired, and the audience kept growing. She ran social media for E! Network's People's Choice Awards under NBCUniversal. She developed campaigns for Keys Soulcare, Alicia Keys' beauty brand, and for MileIQ, a Microsoft productivity product.

In parallel, she built something else: a classroom. For over a decade, Ayanna has taught digital marketing, social media strategy, and business fundamentals at the college level — translating real-world experience into frameworks students could actually use.

Ayanna Henderson Marketing is the agency she built to bring that same level of strategy to every client — whether they're a celebrity brand, a local nonprofit, a national corporation, or a founder ready to grow past where they are.

How We Think

Four beliefs that
drive everything.

01

Strategy before aesthetics.

Design without strategy is decoration. We build marketing frameworks grounded in insight, research, and measurable objectives — then we make them look good.

02

Visibility ≠ scalability.

Going viral is not a strategy. More followers is not a business outcome. We build marketing that converts, retains, and compounds — not marketing that performs for the algorithm and disappears.

03

AI amplifies, humans direct.

We use AI to produce faster, test more, and scale creative — but strategy requires human judgment, cultural fluency, and industry experience that no model can replicate. We use both, intentionally.

04

Small businesses deserve enterprise strategy.

A local restaurant or nonprofit shouldn't settle for generic advice. If you have a real business with real goals, you deserve real strategy — and this agency was built to deliver it regardless of company size.
